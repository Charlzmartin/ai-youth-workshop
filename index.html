⚡ 10 Prompts That Drop Jaws

Copy. Paste. Watch it work. — All free at chat.openai.com or claude.ai

The skill isn't knowing everything. It's knowing how to ask.
1
Instant Résumé Language
"I have experience in [SKILL/JOB]. Write 3 bullet points for my résumé that make me sound highly professional and results-driven."
Turns any work history into polished résumé content in seconds — free résumé writing service.
2
Cover Letter Generator
"Write a cover letter for someone with no degree applying for a [JOB TITLE] position. Make it confident and genuine."
Removes the biggest barrier most young people face when job hunting — what to say.
3
Business Idea Validator
"I want to start a business doing [YOUR IDEA]. Tell me who would pay for this, what I should charge, and how to get my first customer."
Does market research in 10 seconds that would take hours to Google manually.
4
Script for Anything
"Write a 30-second script I can say to introduce myself at a job interview. I want to sound confident but not fake."
Your own personalized interview prep coach — available at 2am the night before.
5
Explain Anything Simply
"Explain [HARD CONCEPT — taxes, credit score, investing] like I'm 15 years old and have never heard of it before."
Turns any confusing adult topic into something finally understandable — without judgment.
6
Social Media Content
"Write 5 Instagram captions for a small business that sells [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Make them engaging, not boring."
This is a service you can sell immediately — small businesses pay $300+/mo for exactly this.
7
Email That Gets Replies
"Write a professional email asking [PERSON/COMPANY] for a job shadow, informational interview, or opportunity. Keep it short and compelling."
Breaks the paralysis of not knowing what to say when reaching out to people who matter.
8
Turn Skills Into a Service
"I'm good at [SKILL]. Help me turn this into a service I can sell online. Give me a name, a description, and a price."
Turns "I'm good at drawing/cooking/fixing things" into a legitimate business offering in minutes.
9
Know Your Rights
"Explain my rights if [SITUATION — pulled over, evicted, fired without warning]. What should I know and what should I do first?"
Access to information that used to cost $300/hr in attorney fees — know before you're in a crisis.
10
Write Your Own Story
"Help me write a 2-minute personal statement about who I am, where I've been, and where I'm going. I want it to sound powerful and real."
The most powerful prompt of all — because your story, told well, opens every door.
